# Audience Personas

## Purpose
Use this skill to clarify who the page or product is for and what matters to them.

This skill helps:
- define target audience segments
- surface pains and motivations
- understand objections
- adapt messaging to relevance
- reduce generic communication

## When to use
Use this skill when:
- the audience is still broad
- messaging feels too generic
- multiple segments may exist
- objections are unclear
- a page needs clearer relevance

## Workflow

### 1. Identify possible segments
Check:
- who is the primary audience
- who is secondary
- who should not be targeted first
- what segment is most valuable now

### 2. Define motivations and pains
Clarify:
- what they want
- what frustrates them
- what outcome they care about
- what they fear or doubt

### 3. Review objections
Check:
- price concerns
- trust concerns
- effort concerns
- timing concerns
- fit concerns

### 4. Translate into messaging needs
Define:
- what tone is appropriate
- what proof matters most
- what claims resonate
- what should be avoided

## Output format
Always structure the answer as:

## Objective
## Primary audience
## Secondary audience
## Pain points and motivations
## Objections
## Messaging implications
## Recommended focus

## Guardrails
- Prioritize one primary audience when possible
- Avoid trying to speak to everyone at once
- Focus on decision-relevant traits
- Keep personas practical, not fictional for the sake of detail
