# Content Briefing

## Purpose
Use this skill to define a clear writing brief before drafting content.

This skill helps:
- clarify the page objective
- identify the audience
- define message priorities
- align writing with user needs
- reduce vague or unfocused copy

## When to use
Use this skill when:
- a new page needs content planning
- writing direction is still unclear
- multiple messages compete for attention
- a writer needs a structured brief
- marketing and design direction exist but writing has not started

## Workflow

### 1. Clarify the page goal
Check:
- what the page must achieve
- what the user should understand
- what action or outcome matters most
- what should not distract from the goal

### 2. Define the audience context
Clarify:
- who the primary reader is
- what they already know
- what they care about most
- what doubts or objections may exist

### 3. Define message priorities
Identify:
- primary message
- supporting messages
- trust or reassurance needs
- CTA or informational next step

### 4. Define writing constraints
Check:
- tone direction
- clarity requirements
- content blocks needed
- dependencies on SEO or design structure

## Output format
Always structure the answer as:

## Objective
## Audience
## Primary message
## Supporting messages
## Tone and writing direction
## Required content blocks
## Risks or gaps
## Next content step

## Guardrails
- Start from user needs, not filler copy
- Keep the brief specific
- Do not mix too many goals into one page
- Make writing priorities explicit
